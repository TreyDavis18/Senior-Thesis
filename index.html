What are Cryptocurrencies?

Cryptocurrencies are a form of decentralized digital currency, meaning that the currency is not stored by any bank or company. Normal currencies are tracked and regulated by banks who will verify transactions to keep an eye on what is going on with the currencies that they regulate. Cryptocurrencies however, use a process of “trustless verification” to verify transactions, meaning that there is no central agency who has the ledger of transactions. The “ledgers”, also referred to as blockchain, for different cryptocurrencies are kept by everyone who takes part in mining each particular cryptocurrency. (3Blue1Brown, 2017) This website will focus on the incredibly popular cryptocurrency called Bitcoin.

Who created Cryptocurrencies?

Cryptocurrencies first made their debut in 2009 with the now insanely popular Bitcoin. No one knows for sure who created Bitcoin, however there is a name attributed to the creation of it. A (claimed) Japanese man named Satoshi Nakamoto is the creator of Bitcoin, however it is widely assumed that this name is a pseudonym and may even be a group of people.

What is mining for Cryptocurrencies?

Mining for Bitcoin is how new Bitcoins are added into circulation. It involves solving equations on a blockchain that are typically encrypted using SHA-256, a type of hash encryption. These equations are public records of transactions using Bitcoin, and solving these equations proves that the transactions are valid. Each block on the blockchain is a group of transaction records that needs to be verified through mining. When a user becomes the first person to solve a transaction block (mine a block) they are awarded with a certain amount of bitcoin.

How does crypto mining work?

Cryptomining utilizes computers containing multiple high-end and extremely powerful graphics cards that work to solve the encrypted transactions on a blockchain. High end GPUs (Graphics Processing Units) are very good at solving thousands of sophisticated algorithms every second, which is exactly what cryptominers need in order to be the first person to solve a transaction record. Because these computer set-ups typically have multiple GPUs running at max power at the same time, they also need a lot of cooling to stop the cards from overheating.

How much energy is used to mine a bitcoin?

Mining for Bitcoin is not an energy efficient process. As of August 2021, around the same amount of energy that a typical household would use in 8 years. This is a little bit better than it was, hitting a max energy consumption of the average household over 13 years in early 2021, but this is still not a sustainable amount of energy for Bitcoin. (Huang et al., 2021)

    • What can be done about the amount of energy that Bitcoin uses?

    Bitcoin uses code that is called “proof of work”. This necessitates a large amount of computers in order to verify and secure the transactions that take place using Bitcoin. One very possible option for Bitcoin is to follow in the path of another highly popular cryptocurrency called Ethereum. Ethereum uses a code process called “proof of stake”. Proof of stake means that miners use their own coins as a pledge to verify any transactions that use Ethereum. This means that if there is a falsified transaction, there are real monetary consequences for the miners. A group made up of Greenpeace USA, Environmental Working Group, and other groups fighting against the environmental effects of cryptomining have stated that changing Bitcoin from proof of work to proof of stake could reduce the energy consumption of Bitcoin by nearly 99%. (Rushe, 2022)
