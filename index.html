Environmental Implications of Cryptomining

Cryptocurrencies are seen as a great way to possibly get rich quick, with rumors of people amassing millions of dollars from mining for various cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin. However, there is a large downside to crypto mining that can not be ignored. Mining for crypto is a very power intensive process and with cryptocurrencies on the rise, more and more people are building mining farms that are drawing in insane amounts of power. Crypto mining involves using incredibly powerful computers containing top of the line GPUs to solve the complex mathematical hash algorithms that are required for unlocking (mining) the next block of crypto coins. The first person to solve the algorithm is rewarded with a black reward that corresponds to an amount of bitcoin. Due to this process rewarding the first person, people are spending more money to build bigger farms, which draw more power, in order to have a higher chance of being first.
